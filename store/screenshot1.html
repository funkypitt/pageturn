PageTurn — Paginated Reading Mode

The Future of Long-Form Reading on the Web

The internet was built for scrolling. But for long articles, research papers, and in-depth journalism, scrolling is a poor reading experience. Our eyes lose their place. Our thumbs get tired. And on e-ink displays, every scroll means a full-screen refresh that breaks concentration.

PageTurn solves this by converting any web article into clean, paginated screens — just like a book. One tap or keypress swaps to the next page instantly, with no animation and no scrolling. The text reflows perfectly to fill your screen, whether you're on a desktop monitor or a tablet.

How It Works

The extension automatically detects article content on popular platforms like Substack, Medium, WordPress, and Ghost. It strips away navigation bars, sidebars, subscribe prompts, and ads — leaving only the text you came to read. Content is reflowed using CSS multi-column layout, ensuring no line is ever cut in half between pages.

Reading on an e-ink tablet finally feels natural. No more fighting with scroll-based layouts that cause constant screen refreshes.

Navigation is intuitive: arrow keys or swipe gestures move between pages. A small page counter in the corner tells you exactly where you are. Press Escape or tap the close button to restore the original page.
